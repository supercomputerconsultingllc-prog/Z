## Case Study 1 — Expansion Plan Stress Test for a Colocation Operator

**Client profile**  
Regional colocation operator preparing a multi-million-dollar expansion in an existing facility.

**Challenge**  
Leadership was close to approving expansion spend, but key assumptions around capacity growth, resiliency targets, sequencing, and operational support had not been pressure-tested from an owner-side perspective. The risk was not just overbuilding — it was locking in design choices that would create long-term operating complexity and avoidable retrofit cost.

**Approach**  
Supercomputer Consulting reviewed the proposed expansion through an operations-and-risk lens rather than a design-only lens. The engagement focused on:

- Critical infrastructure assumptions and capacity planning logic
- Resiliency objectives versus actual business requirements
- Phasing and constructability constraints in a live environment
- Operational support implications for staffing, maintenance, and change management
- Likely decision points where early clarification could prevent later rework

The output was an executive-level decision framework supported by technical observations, risk flags, and practical recommendations.

**Outcome**  
The client moved forward with a clearer scope, better sequencing assumptions, and stronger internal alignment on what the expansion actually needed to accomplish. Leadership was able to challenge potential overbuild, surface hidden operational burdens before construction, and make capital decisions with more confidence.

## Case Study 2 — Operational Risk Review for a Mission-Critical Enterprise Facility

**Client profile**  
Enterprise operator running a business-critical data center environment with low tolerance for disruption.

**Challenge**  
Leadership wanted an independent view of reliability exposure beyond what internal reporting and routine maintenance reviews were showing. The concern was that documented procedures, staffing practices, escalation paths, and maintenance execution might not be sufficiently aligned to the facility’s actual risk profile.

**Approach**  
Supercomputer Consulting performed a focused assessment of operational resilience, including:

- Preventive maintenance discipline and follow-through
- Operating procedures and change control maturity
- Escalation clarity during abnormal events
- Roles, staffing dependencies, and coverage assumptions
- Physical and procedural single points of failure
- Gaps between stated resiliency posture and day-to-day practice

Findings were organized by materiality so leadership could distinguish immediate reliability concerns from longer-horizon process improvements.

**Outcome**  
The client received a prioritized roadmap for reducing operational risk without turning the exercise into a generic compliance checklist. Management gained better visibility into which weaknesses were most likely to contribute to avoidable incidents, where discipline needed to tighten, and how to phase improvements realistically.

- High-risk items closed in first 90 days: [# or %]
- PM completion improvement: [%]

## Case Study 3 — Modernization Strategy for an Aging Facility Near Capacity

**Client profile**  
Owner or operator of an aging data center facing infrastructure limitations, rising business demands, and pressure to avoid a disruptive wrong turn.

**Challenge**  
The client needed to decide whether to extend the useful life of the existing facility, modernize selectively, reconfigure for different operating priorities, or begin planning for a larger transition. The issue was not simply technical condition — it was how to align limited capital with business needs, resilience expectations, and operational reality.

**Approach**  
Supercomputer Consulting evaluated the environment in terms of:

- Current infrastructure condition and practical remaining life
- Capacity constraints across power, cooling, and support systems
- Operational pain points and maintenance burden
- Business growth requirements and service continuity needs
- Tradeoffs between patching, phased modernization, and larger strategic moves

Instead of producing a one-dimensional recommendation, the engagement framed realistic options, dependencies, and likely consequences for each path.

**Outcome**  
Stakeholders gained a structured basis for investment planning and a more disciplined way to compare short-term fixes against longer-term strategy. The client was able to focus leadership discussion on the highest-value modernization moves rather than defaulting to either excessive spending or incremental deferral.

- Deferred capital reprioritized: [$]
- Infrastructure capacity gained: [kW / MW / %]
- Maintenance burden reduced: [% or directional]

## Case Study 4 — Technical and Operational Due Diligence for an Investor-Led Decision

**Client profile**  
Investor, buyer, or executive team evaluating a data center-related acquisition, lease, or strategic commitment.

**Challenge**  
The client needed more than a high-level site tour or engineering summary. They wanted an independent view of facility condition, operational maturity, resilience posture, and likely remediation exposure before making a meaningful financial decision.

**Approach**  
Supercomputer Consulting conducted a diligence review centered on business risk as much as technical status, including:

- Facility infrastructure condition and apparent weak points
- Operational maturity and process discipline
- Reliability posture relative to intended use case
- Hidden remediation or upgrade exposure likely to affect economics
- Executive-level framing of issues that mattered most to the decision

The final deliverable emphasized practical implications: what required deeper investigation, what might change the investment thesis, and where assumptions should be challenged before closing.

**Outcome**  
The client entered decision-making with a clearer understanding of downside exposure, operational limitations, and probable follow-on cost drivers. That clarity improved executive discussions around valuation, risk allocation, and post-transaction priorities.

## Case Study 5 — Owner-Side Review to Improve Design Before It Was Locked In

**Client profile**  
Organization planning a new build, retrofit, or major infrastructure upgrade with external design and delivery partners already involved.

**Challenge**  
The client wanted experienced owner-side oversight before key technical decisions became expensive to change. They were concerned that the design process could optimize for paper compliance or consultant convention without fully reflecting operational maintainability, real failure modes, or day-two support requirements.

**Approach**  
Supercomputer Consulting reviewed the developing plan with attention to:

- Alignment between business intent, resiliency goals, and actual design choices
- Maintainability and operability in a live mission-critical setting
- Assumptions that could create future maintenance or outage risk
- Places where owner interests needed stronger technical challenge
- Coordination gaps between design intent and operational execution

Rather than replacing the design team, the role was to strengthen the client’s position by surfacing questions early and translating technical concerns into actionable decision points.

**Outcome**  
The client improved decision quality before major commitments were finalized. Potentially costly blind spots were identified earlier, design discussions became more grounded in operational reality, and leadership gained more confidence that the project would serve long-term needs rather than just satisfy short-term milestones.

## Case Study 6 — AI / HPC Readiness Assessment for a Higher-Density Growth Plan

**Client profile**  
Enterprise, operator, or provider evaluating whether an existing facility could support higher-density AI or HPC deployment requirements.

**Challenge**  
The client saw commercial opportunity in higher-density workloads but lacked confidence that current power, cooling, floor layout, operational procedures, and resilience assumptions could support the target environment without unacceptable cost or reliability risk.

**Approach**  
Supercomputer Consulting assessed readiness across both infrastructure and operations, including:

- Power path and cooling constraints relevant to denser deployments
- Capacity bottlenecks likely to limit phased rollout
- Practical retrofit implications in an occupied environment
- Operating model implications for maintenance, monitoring, and response
- Decision paths for phased upgrades versus larger strategic changes

The assessment focused on what the facility could realistically support, what would need to change first, and where leadership should be cautious about overcommitting.

**Outcome**  
The client gained a practical roadmap for evaluating higher-density growth without relying on assumptions alone. Leadership was better equipped to distinguish achievable near-term upgrades from longer-term infrastructure requirements and to set expectations around cost, risk, and implementation sequencing.
