# Supercomputer Consulting — Lead Generation Playbook

## Objective
Create a focused, high-trust pipeline for premium advisory work in data center diligence, resiliency, modernization, owner-side advisory, and AI / HPC readiness.

The operating principle: **target fewer, better accounts and run a disciplined follow-up system.**

---

## Ideal buyer segments

### Segment 1 — Private equity, infrastructure funds, and strategic investors
**Why they buy**
- They face time-sensitive diligence windows
- Hidden facility risk can materially change valuation
- They need an independent perspective separate from sellers, brokers, and design teams

**Titles to target**
- Partner
- Managing Director
- Principal
- Vice President
- Director, Infrastructure Investments
- Operating Partner
- Portfolio Operations Leader

**Primary triggers**
- Acquisition in progress
- Platform investment thesis in digital infrastructure
- Portfolio company expansion or remediation issues
- New fund or active deal activity

**Offer angles**
- Independent technical / operational diligence
- Facility risk review before closing
- Post-acquisition remediation prioritization

### Segment 2 — Data center owners, operators, and colocation providers
**Why they buy**
- They need outside judgment on resiliency, modernization, and operating blind spots
- Expansion and retrofit mistakes are expensive
- Executive teams often want an owner-side advisor, not another vendor pitch

**Titles to target**
- COO
- CTO
- SVP Infrastructure
- VP Operations
- VP Data Center Operations
- Director of Critical Facilities
- Director of Data Center Operations
- Head of Facilities Engineering

**Primary triggers**
- Capacity expansion
- Reliability incidents or recurring operational concerns
- Retrofit / modernization program
- Leadership transition
- New market entry

**Offer angles**
- Operational risk and resiliency assessment
- Owner-side review of expansion / retrofit plans
- Executive advisory on modernization tradeoffs

### Segment 3 — Enterprise infrastructure and mission-critical facility leaders
**Why they buy**
- They need independent evaluation before committing capital
- Their internal teams may be strong but still benefit from outside challenge
- High-availability facilities have asymmetric downside when decisions go wrong

**Titles to target**
- VP Infrastructure
- Director of Infrastructure
- Director of Data Centers
- Head of Critical Facilities
- VP Technology Operations
- Facilities Engineering Leader

**Primary triggers**
- Facility modernization planning
- Consolidation or relocation
- Reliability concerns
- Large refresh or power/cooling upgrade decisions

**Offer angles**
- Independent operational review
- Modernization planning support
- Executive decision support for high-risk infrastructure choices

### Segment 4 — AI / HPC infrastructure teams
**Why they buy**
- Density, power, and cooling assumptions can break quickly
- Leadership needs practical readiness guidance before deployment commitments
- Facilities and compute teams are often misaligned on constraints

**Titles to target**
- Head of AI Infrastructure
- Head of Compute Infrastructure
- Director of Data Center Strategy
- Capacity Planning Leader
- Facilities Program Manager
- Director of Infrastructure Strategy

**Primary triggers**
- GPU / AI cluster plans
- New HPC workload expansion
- Power or cooling bottlenecks
- Board-level AI initiative

**Offer angles**
- AI / HPC facility readiness assessment
- Upgrade prioritization
- Constraint and sequencing review

### Segment 5 — Referral partners
These are not always direct buyers, but they can produce qualified introductions.

**Who to build with**
- Commissioning agents
- MEP consultants
- Specialized contractors
- Recruiters in mission-critical infrastructure
- Infrastructure attorneys
- Project / capital advisors
- Equipment service firms

**Goal**
Become the independent operator-grounded advisor they think of when a client needs owner-side judgment.

## Account segmentation model

### Tier A — High-probability, high-value accounts
Criteria:
- Clear fit with one of the core offers
- Likely active project or transaction in the next 6 months
- Senior decision-maker identifiable
- Estimated opportunity value justifies personalized pursuit

**Action standard:** full research + personalized multi-touch sequence

### Tier B — Strong fit, lower certainty
Criteria:
- Good account profile but weaker timing signal
- Limited access to decision-makers
- Potential referral or later-stage opportunity

**Action standard:** lighter personalization + nurture sequence

### Tier C — Strategic network accounts
Criteria:
- Valuable ecosystem player
- Potential referral source
- Useful for intelligence, introductions, or credibility building

**Action standard:** relationship-first outreach, not hard selling

## Target list buildout

### Initial named-account goal
Build a starting list of **60 accounts**:
- 15 investors / PE / infrastructure funds
- 20 owners / operators / colocation accounts
- 15 enterprise mission-critical organizations
- 10 AI / HPC aligned accounts

For each account capture:
- Account name
- Segment
- Tier (A/B/C)
- Geography
- Why it fits
- Trigger / current initiative
- 2–5 target contacts
- LinkedIn / source links
- Last touch date
- Next step
- Status

### Minimum contact standard per account
Each A-tier account should have:
- 1 likely decision-maker
- 1 likely influencer / technical leader
- 1 backup stakeholder

## Messaging pillars
All outreach should stay anchored to a small set of themes.

1. **Independent perspective**  
   Not selling equipment, design services, or implementation labor.

2. **Risk reduction**  
   The value is finding what leadership could miss before it becomes expensive.

3. **Decision quality**  
   Better judgment, better sequencing, fewer blind spots.

4. **Operational realism**  
   Bridge paper plans and real-world operating conditions.

5. **High-stakes timing**  
   Most valuable before the commitment is irreversible.

## Core call-to-action ladder
Use the smallest reasonable next step.

### Primary CTA
- 20-minute fit / discovery call

### Secondary CTA
- Send one-page overview
- Send relevant article or checklist
- Brief exchange by email if timing is early

### Core offers to introduce after interest
- Data center due diligence advisory
- Operational risk / resiliency assessment
- Owner’s representative advisory
- AI / HPC readiness assessment
- Fractional executive advisory

## Outreach cadence

### Cadence A — Cold targeted outbound
**Day 1:** Personalized email  
**Day 3:** LinkedIn connection request  
**Day 7:** Follow-up email with sharper angle or useful content  
**Day 12:** LinkedIn follow-up or voicemail if appropriate  
**Day 18:** Final close-the-loop email  
**Day 30+:** Move to nurture and re-approach on a new trigger

### Cadence B — Warm intro / referral
**Day 1:** Reply within 24 hours  
**Day 2–4:** Offer 2–3 meeting windows  
**Day 7:** Send one-page overview and confirm problem statement  
**Day 14:** Follow up with a point of view tied to their project

### Cadence C — Trigger-based
Use when you see:
- Acquisition activity
- Expansion announcement
- Funding event
- Leadership change
- Reliability incident
- AI initiative or GPU deployment announcement
- Major capital project announcement

**Response window:** within 24–72 hours

## Channel mix

### Email
Best for direct, concise, low-friction outreach.

### LinkedIn
Best for warming up cold accounts, reinforcing credibility, and light follow-up.

### Referrals / partner introductions
Best source of premium opportunities; should be cultivated weekly.

### Content
Supports trust, authority, and follow-up quality. Content should help convert skeptical but relevant buyers.

## Weekly KPI dashboard
Track a handful of numbers, not dozens.

### Activity KPIs
- New target accounts added
- New contacts added
- Personalized emails sent
- LinkedIn connection requests sent
- Follow-ups sent
- Referral asks made
- Content posts published

### Funnel KPIs
- Positive reply rate
- Discovery calls booked
- Discovery-to-qualified rate
- Qualified-to-proposal rate
- Proposal win rate
- Average deal size
- Days from first touch to proposal

### Suggested weekly targets for a solo advisory practice
- 8–12 new accounts researched
- 15–20 new contacts added
- 12–18 personalized outbound emails
- 8–12 LinkedIn connection requests
- 10–15 follow-ups
- 2 referral asks
- 1 authority post or article excerpt
- 2–4 discovery calls booked per month

### Healthy benchmark ranges
- Cold email positive reply rate: **5–12%**
- Discovery booking rate from engaged leads: **25–50%**
- Discovery to qualified opportunity: **40–70%**
- Proposal win rate on qualified opportunities: **25–50%**

If metrics fall below this, improve targeting and relevance before increasing volume.

---

## Qualification criteria
A lead is likely worth pursuing when at least 3–4 of these are true:
- There is a real decision, transaction, or project underway
- Timing is within 1–9 months
- A budget path exists
- Executive sponsorship or decision-maker access exists
- The downside of getting it wrong is material
- They value independent judgment over cheapest hourly labor

### Fast disqualifiers
- They want generic IT help
- No clear decision exists
- No budget path and no sponsor
- They want free design work or broad unpaid brainstorming
- They are clearly shopping lowest-cost commodity consulting

## Weekly execution rhythm

### Monday — Build and prioritize
- Review pipeline by stage
- Identify top 10 accounts for this week
- Add new target accounts and contacts
- Choose any trigger-based outreach

### Tuesday — First-touch outbound
- Send personalized emails to the week’s priority contacts
- Send LinkedIn connection requests
- Log all touches immediately

### Wednesday — Follow-up + referral work
- Follow up on prior touches older than 4–5 business days
- Ask 1–2 partners or contacts for introductions
- Engage with relevant LinkedIn posts from target accounts

### Thursday — Discovery and proposal prep
- Run discovery calls
- Write follow-up summaries
- Advance qualified opportunities to scoped next steps

### Friday — Content + review
- Publish one useful insight post
- Review KPI dashboard
- Note which messages / segments performed best
- Queue next week’s top targets

---

## 30-minute daily operating checklist
- Check replies and LinkedIn responses
- Move leads to the correct CRM stage
- Schedule next follow-up for every active lead
- Send today’s touches
- Record lessons from calls

If a lead has no next step scheduled, it is likely to stall.

---

## CRM stage definitions
1. **Target identified** — account fits ICP, no outreach yet
2. **Contact researched** — decision-makers added
3. **Initial outreach sent** — first touch complete
4. **Engaged** — replied, accepted connection, or referred in
5. **Discovery scheduled** — meeting confirmed
6. **Qualified opportunity** — clear need, timing, sponsor, budget path
7. **Proposal / scope sent** — next-step document delivered
8. **Closed won**
9. **Closed lost**
10. **Nurture** — fit exists, timing weak
