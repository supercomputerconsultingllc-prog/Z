## Qualification rubric
Use a simple five-factor rubric so every inquiry is judged the same way.

### Factor 1 — Problem clarity
**0 points:** vague, no real issue described  
**1 point:** broad interest but unclear use case  
**2 points:** clear project or decision described  
**3 points:** urgent, specific, high-stakes issue clearly articulated

### Factor 2 — Strategic fit
**0 points:** unrelated to core services  
**1 point:** partial fit  
**2 points:** strong match to one core offer  
**3 points:** direct match to premium advisory work

### Factor 3 — Timing
**0 points:** no timing / “someday”  
**1 point:** 6+ months or exploratory  
**2 points:** 1–6 months  
**3 points:** immediate or active project / transaction

### Factor 4 — Stakeholder quality
**0 points:** unknown or junior contact only  
**1 point:** influencer but weak buying power  
**2 points:** sponsor or strong internal advocate  
**3 points:** decision-maker or direct access to one

### Factor 5 — Commercial viability
**0 points:** no budget path, obvious price-shopper  
**1 point:** unclear budget but possible path  
**2 points:** budget likely or partially approved  
**3 points:** clear budget or high willingness to fund external help

### Maximum score
15 points

---

## Lead scoring interpretation

### 12–15 points — High priority
- Respond same business day if possible
- Offer a 20-minute discovery call
- Prepare segment-specific follow-up
- Add to active pipeline immediately

### 8–11 points — Medium priority
- Respond within 1 business day
- Ask 2–4 clarifying questions if needed
- Offer overview + call
- Keep in working pipeline

### 5–7 points — Low priority / nurture
- Send polite response
- Offer overview or resource
- Move to nurture unless new information improves score

### 0–4 points — Poor fit
- Decline cleanly or redirect
- Do not invest heavy time

---

## Hard qualification rules
Regardless of score, move cautiously or disqualify if:
- The inquiry is for generic IT support
- They want free design / engineering work
- The request is clearly commodity staffing or implementation labor
- There is no real project, sponsor, or business issue
- The tone suggests pure price-shopping for low-cost hours

---

## Manual review checklist
Before replying, confirm:
- Is the company real and relevant?
- Does the title suggest influence or buying power?
- Is the problem aligned to diligence, resiliency, modernization, owner-side advisory, or AI / HPC readiness?
- Is there enough urgency to justify a live conversation?
- Is the likely next step a call, a clarifying email, or a polite decline?

---

## Sample intake review workflow

### High-priority inbound
1. Review form and company background
2. Score lead
3. Personalize response
4. Offer call windows or booking link
5. Create CRM record
6. Prepare discovery notes

### Medium-priority inbound
1. Review form
2. Score lead
3. Send brief clarifying email
4. Promote to discovery if reply is strong
5. Otherwise nurture

### Low-priority inbound
1. Review quickly
2. Send helpful but bounded reply
3. Attach overview if useful
4. Archive or nurture
