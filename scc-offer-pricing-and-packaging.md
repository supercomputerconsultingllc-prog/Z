# Supercomputer Consulting — Offer, Pricing, and Packaging Strategy

## Purpose
This document defines how Supercomputer Consulting should package and present premium advisory services so they feel senior, outcome-oriented, and easy to buy. The objective is not to maximize menu size. The objective is to make high-value engagements legible, credible, and commercially strong.

---

## 1. Packaging philosophy
SCC should package around **decisions and risk**, not around generic hours.

Clients buy when they believe:
- the issue is consequential,
- the advisor is independent,
- the work will reduce uncertainty,
- and the scope is clear enough to approve quickly.

That means the offer architecture should:
- start with sharply defined flagship engagements,
- escalate naturally into longer advisory work,
- avoid commodity-feeling hourly language,
- and give buyers a premium but understandable path to say yes.

---

## 2. Offer ladder
Use a simple ladder with increasing commitment and value.

### Level 1 — Diagnostic / decision-entry offers
These are the easiest premium entry points. They should be fast to understand and directly tied to a live issue.

#### Offer A: Data Center Due Diligence Review
**Best for** investors, acquirers, portfolio teams, enterprise buyers

**When to lead with it**
- acquisition or investment review
- major lease / capacity decision
- facility purchase or strategic commitment

**Core promise**
Get an independent view of facility, operational, and remediation risk before capital is locked in.

**Packaging notes**
- fixed-fee, tightly scoped
- accelerated option for live transactions
- positioned as decision support, not generic site inspection

---

#### Offer B: Operational Risk & Resiliency Assessment
**Best for** operators, enterprise owners, colocation providers

**When to lead with it**
- reliability concerns
- modernization planning
- executive concern about hidden failure exposure
- pre-customer or pre-board readiness review

**Core promise**
Identify the operational, maintenance, and resiliency weaknesses most likely to create expensive consequences.

**Packaging notes**
- fixed-fee assessment
- report + executive debrief
- strong bridge into roadmap or retained advisory

---

#### Offer C: AI / HPC Facility Readiness Assessment
**Best for** AI infrastructure teams, research/HPC environments, providers adapting for high-density loads

**When to lead with it**
- GPU cluster plans
- facility uncertainty around density, cooling, or power
- phased deployment sequencing decisions

**Core promise**
Clarify whether the current facility can support the roadmap, what constraints matter most, and what must change first.

**Packaging notes**
- fixed-fee, decision-oriented
- should produce a clear go / no-go / phase-plan recommendation

---

### Level 2 — Project-embedded advisory offers
These are higher-value engagements for clients already facing complex execution risk.

#### Offer D: Owner’s Representative Advisory
**Best for** expansions, retrofits, modernization, new builds, high-stakes upgrades

**Core promise**
Protect the owner’s interest across design, coordination, technical decisions, and risk escalation.

**Packaging notes**
- milestone-based or monthly retainer
- limited number of clients at once to preserve senior attention
- positioned as executive-grade owner-side oversight, not project administration

---

#### Offer E: Modernization Strategy & Decision Roadmap
**Best for** aging facilities, capital planning, replace-versus-upgrade questions

**Core promise**
Give leadership a practical roadmap for where to invest, sequence work, and avoid expensive partial fixes.

**Packaging notes**
- fixed-fee strategy engagement
- can be sold as a follow-on to resiliency assessment
- should emphasize tradeoffs and decision sequencing

---

### Level 3 — Ongoing executive advisory
This is the highest-trust, highest-margin layer once SCC has earned a seat at the table.

#### Offer F: Fractional Executive / Strategic Advisory Retainer
**Best for** owner-operators, PE-backed platforms, enterprise leaders in transition, growth-stage infrastructure teams

**Core promise**
Provide senior-level judgment for recurring infrastructure, vendor, modernization, and risk decisions without a full-time executive hire.

**Packaging notes**
- monthly retainer
- defined advisory cadence and access boundaries
- can include board/executive prep, vendor review, escalation support, and major-decision counseling

---

## 3. Recommended flagship offers
To avoid a cluttered market position, SCC should emphasize four visible flagship offers externally:

1. **Data Center Due Diligence Review**
2. **Operational Risk & Resiliency Assessment**
3. **Owner’s Representative Advisory**
4. **AI / HPC Facility Readiness Assessment**

Then keep these as secondary / follow-on offers:
- Modernization Strategy & Decision Roadmap
- Fractional Executive / Strategic Advisory Retainer

Why this works:
- the first four are easier to understand,
- each maps cleanly to a buyer trigger,
- and the secondary offers feel like natural upgrades rather than unrelated services.

---

## 4. Offer structure template
Every premium SCC offer should follow the same commercial structure.

### A. Trigger
What situation makes this urgent now?

### B. Problem statement
What risk, uncertainty, or hidden cost is the client dealing with?

### C. Scope of review
What exactly will SCC assess, challenge, or guide?

### D. Deliverables
What concrete output will the buyer receive?

### E. Decision value
What will leadership be able to decide more confidently after the work?

### F. Next-step path
If the client wants more help, what is the logical follow-on engagement?

This structure keeps offers outcome-led instead of sounding like open-ended consulting time.

## 5. Pricing presentation strategy
SCC should present pricing in a way that reinforces value and seniority.

### Core principle
**Price the engagement, not the labor.**

Clients should understand that they are buying:
- judgment,
- independence,
- pattern recognition,
- risk reduction,
- and faster, better decisions.

### Best-practice pricing language
Use:
- “fixed-fee advisory engagement”
- “project fee based on scope, complexity, and decision timeline”
- “monthly advisory retainer”
- “accelerated diligence pricing for compressed transaction timelines”

Avoid leading with:
- “hourly consulting”
- “day rate”
- “time and materials,” unless required for edge cases

### Why
Hourly framing invites price comparison with lower-value providers and shifts attention away from consequence, speed, and judgment.

## 6. Suggested pricing architecture
Exact numbers will depend on market, reputation, and founder proof points, but the structure should look like this.

### Diagnostic fixed-fee band
Use for due diligence, resiliency assessments, AI/HPC readiness, and modernization strategy.

Pricing should vary by:
- facility count and complexity,
- depth of documentation review,
- on-site versus remote work,
- number of stakeholder interviews,
- turnaround speed,
- and importance of executive synthesis.

### Retainer band
Use for owner’s rep and fractional strategic advisory.

Pricing should vary by:
- meeting cadence,
- expected responsiveness,
- number of active projects,
- travel burden,
- depth of review responsibility,
- and whether SCC is acting as a visible executive advisor versus behind-the-scenes sounding board.

### Add-on pricing elements
Add premiums for:
- compressed timelines,
- multi-site scope,
- extensive document review,
- workshop facilitation,
- board / investor presentation support,
- and travel-intensive work.

This lets SCC protect margin without overcomplicating base packages.

## 7. Three ways to show price without commoditizing

### Option 1 — Custom-scoped only
Best when founder credibility is strong and buyers are enterprise-grade.

**Presentation**
“Engagements are scoped based on facility complexity, decision urgency, and the level of review required.”

**Use when**
- most work is bespoke,
- scope variability is high,
- and SCC wants maximum pricing flexibility.

---

### Option 2 — Starting-from price bands
Best when SCC wants more transparency without locking itself into commodity pricing.

**Presentation examples**
- Due diligence reviews begin at a defined minimum for single-site scopes
- Resiliency assessments start at a defined range depending on site complexity
- Owner’s rep engagements are quoted as monthly advisory retainers

**Use when**
- prospects need some budget calibration,
- but SCC still wants room to price for complexity.

---

### Option 3 — Good / better / best packaging
Best when buyers need a clearer commercial decision and SCC wants to anchor upward.

**Example structure for an assessment**
- **Core Review:** focused assessment + executive summary
- **Expanded Review:** deeper stakeholder interviews + prioritized roadmap
- **Executive Package:** expanded review + workshop / presentation + follow-on advisory checkpoint

**Use when**
- the same basic problem recurs across accounts,
- and SCC wants to increase average deal size without feeling pushy.

---

## 9. Deliverable strategy
Premium buyers should leave with outputs that can be used in real decision forums.

### Standard deliverable types
- executive summary,
- prioritized risk register,
- decision memo,
- remediation / modernization roadmap,
- stakeholder debrief,
- board or investor presentation support,
- checkpoint session after delivery.

### Important rule
Do not overproduce technical paperwork that feels like generic engineering documentation. Deliverables should help leadership make decisions, align stakeholders, and prioritize action.

---

## 10. Proposal strategy
SCC proposals should be short, executive-friendly, and clearly commercial.

### Recommended sections
1. client situation and decision context
2. objectives of the engagement
3. scope and approach
4. deliverables and timing
5. client responsibilities / assumptions
6. fee and payment structure
7. optional follow-on support

### Tone
Calm, direct, premium. No bloated consulting jargon.

### Commercial objective
Make the proposal feel like a straightforward path to de-risking an important decision.

---

## 11. Upsell and expansion logic
The cleanest growth path is to move clients up the ladder after a successful initial engagement.

### Natural expansion paths
- Due diligence review → post-close risk roadmap → fractional advisory
- Resiliency assessment → modernization roadmap → owner’s rep advisory
- AI/HPC readiness → phased upgrade strategy → embedded advisory
- Owner’s rep engagement → ongoing strategic advisory retainer

### Principle
Do not force the upsell during the initial sale. Instead, design the first engagement so the need for the next step becomes obvious from the findings.

---

## 12. Guardrails for premium positioning

### Do
- lead with business consequence,
- use fixed-fee or retainer language,
- show a clear decision outcome,
- keep the menu tight,
- maintain senior-level scarcity.

### Do not
- list dozens of service variants,
- publish bargain pricing,
- frame the firm as generic engineering labor,
- promise unlimited access under a flat retainer,
- let proposals become sprawling unpaid consulting.

---

## 13. Recommended external pricing language
Use concise language like:

- “Most engagements are scoped as fixed-fee advisory projects based on facility complexity, decision urgency, and review depth.”
- “For ongoing owner-side or executive support, we typically structure monthly advisory retainers.”
- “Accelerated transaction timelines, multi-site scopes, and board-level support are priced accordingly.”

This gives clarity without forcing commodity comparison.

---

## 14. Blunt recommendation
SCC should resist the temptation to sell broad expertise as an unlimited service catalog. The stronger move is:
- four clear flagship offers,
- premium fixed-fee entry points,
- retainer-based follow-on work,
- and pricing framed around consequence, complexity, and speed.

That is how an experienced data center advisor looks expensive in the right way rather than vague in the wrong way.
