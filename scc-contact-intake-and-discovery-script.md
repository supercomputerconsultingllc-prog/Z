## Discovery Call Goals

The call is not to impress the prospect with jargon.

The call should answer:

1. Is there a real business problem or decision in front of them?
2. Is the problem high-value and urgent enough to justify advisory support?
3. Is Supercomputer Consulting the right fit?
4. What is the most sensible next step: proposal, follow-up, referral, or no-fit?

---

## 20-Minute Discovery Call Structure

### 1. Opening (2 minutes)

"Thanks for making time. I’d like to understand the decision or challenge in front of you, ask a few questions about timing and scope, and then I can tell you whether I think there’s a fit and what a sensible next step would look like."

### 2. Situation (5 minutes)
Ask:
- What is happening right now that prompted the conversation?
- What decision are you trying to make or what problem are you trying to solve?
- Is this tied to an acquisition, expansion, modernization, reliability concern, or AI / HPC growth?

### 3. Risk and stakes (4 minutes)
Ask:
- What are the biggest uncertainties or risks as you see them?
- What happens if this goes poorly or gets delayed?
- What would leadership want to avoid at all costs?

### 4. Scope and context (4 minutes)
Ask:
- What type of facility or portfolio is involved?
- Who are the main stakeholders?
- What work has already been done?
- Are there outside firms or internal teams already involved?

### 5. Timing and budget (3 minutes)
Ask:
- What timeline are you working against?
- Is there already an approved budget or a path to one?
- What does a good outcome from outside advisory support look like?

### 6. Close (2 minutes)

"Based on what you’ve shared, I see [fit / possible fit / not the right fit]. The next sensible step would be [proposal / scoped follow-up / review of documents / referral / no further action]."

## Short Qualification Questions

Use these in forms, email replies, or quick calls:

- What decision is in front of you right now?
- What kind of facility or environment is involved?
- What are the top risks or unknowns?
- What is your timing?
- Who needs to sign off?
- Is there budget approval or executive sponsorship?

## Red Flags to Watch

- No specific decision or project exists
- They want generic IT help, not mission-critical advisory
- They want free consulting before any scope discussion
- No decision-maker is involved
- Timeline is imaginary and budget is absent

---

## Green Flags to Watch

- Active transaction, expansion, or modernization project
- Reliability concern with real cost exposure
- Executive sponsor is engaged
- Prospect values outside perspective and judgment
- There is urgency, budget, and a concrete next decision
